## **Optimized Prompt for Sentiment Analysis**

**Primary Instruction:**
Classify the sentiment of the following text as **Positive**, **Neutral**, or **Negative**. Apply these specific guidelines:

**1. Financial/Business Context Rules:**
- Treat standalone financial metrics (revenues, profits, margins, losses) as **NEUTRAL** unless accompanied by:
  - Comparative language ("increased from," "fell short of," "beat expectations")
  - Explicit evaluative words ("strong," "disappointing," "record high")
  - Contextual benchmarks vs. previous periods or forecasts
- Interpret reduced losses, cost synergies, and strategic refocusing as **POSITIVE** in business contexts
- Consider high/sustained margins in commodity markets as **POSITIVE** for sellers
- Treat corporate actions (joint ventures, expansions, profit distributions) as **NEUTRAL** when factually reported without evaluation

**2. Sentiment Determination Framework:**
- **Positive**: Contains explicit positive language OR describes favorable outcomes/improvements
- **Neutral**: Factual statements without subjective evaluation OR mixed/balanced information
- **Negative**: Contains explicit negative language OR describes unfavorable outcomes/declines

**3. Key Decision Factors:**
- Prioritize explicit emotional/evaluative language over implied sentiment
- For factual business statements, require clear positive/negative framing beyond raw numbers
- Consider domain context: operational readiness statements after scrutiny = positive; strategic refocusing = positive
- When comparing numerical values, interpret improvement (smaller losses, higher margins) as positive

**4. Examples for Reference:**
- **Positive**: "Profit soared 50% above expectations to record levels"
- **Neutral**: "Q4 revenue was $500M with an 8% operating margin"
- **Negative**: "Losses deepened to $10M despite cost-cutting efforts"
- **Positive**: "The reduced loss of $0.4M shows significant improvement from last quarter"
- **Neutral**: "The board proposed a profit distribution of $0.92 per share"
- **Positive**: "All safety systems are fully operational following the inspection"

   - Reduced losses = positive
   - Strategic refocusing = positive  
   - High margins = positive (in commodity contexts)
   - Operational readiness after scrutiny = positive

3. **Clear Decision Hierarchy**: Establishes that explicit language trumps implied sentiment, preventing over-interpretation of neutral business facts.

4. **Contrastive Examples**: Provides clear examples that demonstrate the distinction between similar-looking statements with different sentiment labels.

5. **Three-Part Framework**: Separates rules by (1) context, (2) framework, (3) decision factors for systematic application.
